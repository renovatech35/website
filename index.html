Environmental Policy

Last updated: November 2025 | Location: Victoria, Australia

Zero Landfill

We aim to divert 100% of electronic waste from landfills.

Sustainability

Supporting a circular economy through reuse and material recovery.

Compliance

Strict adherence to Victorian and Australian environmental laws.

At RenovaTech, we are committed to managing IT assets in a way that protects the environment, supports a circular economy, and complies with Australian environmental laws. As an IT Asset Disposition (ITAD) company, we understand the impact that electronic waste can have, and we aim to minimise it at every stage.

1. Our Environmental Commitment

RenovaTech is committed to:

  • Reducing the amount of e-waste sent to landfill
  • Maximising reuse, refurbishment, and recovery of IT equipment
  • Handling hazardous materials safely and responsibly
  • Complying with all relevant environmental regulations, including:
    • Environment Protection Act 2017 (VIC)
    • Environment Protection Regulations 2021 (VIC)
    • AS/NZS 5377:2013 E-Waste Management Standard

We regularly review our processes to find better, more sustainable ways of operating.

2. Responsible E-Waste Management

We manage e-waste through a structured and responsible process:

  • Sorting & Assessment – Devices are inspected and sorted into categories (reuse, parts, recycling, hazardous).
  • Specialist Recycling – We work with EPA-compliant recycling partners for the recovery of metals, plastics, circuit boards and other components.
  • Hazardous Materials – Items such as lithium batteries, CRTs and other hazardous components are stored, transported and disposed of in line with regulatory requirements.

3. Refurbishment & Reuse

We follow a “Reuse First” approach wherever possible:

  • Devices suitable for reuse are tested, repaired, cleaned and securely wiped (in line with NIST 800-88).
  • Refurbished equipment may be resold or, where appropriate, donated to approved community organisations, schools or charities.
  • Only devices that cannot be reused or repaired move to materials recovery and recycling.

4. Reducing Waste

Within our operations we aim to:

  • Reduce unnecessary packaging
  • Reuse packaging materials where practical
  • Recycle cardboard, plastics and metals from our facilities
  • Encourage clients to use bulk or scheduled collections to reduce the number of trips required

5. Transport & Logistics

To reduce the environmental impact of transport, RenovaTech:

  • Plans efficient collection routes
  • Groups pickups where possible
  • Maintains vehicles to improve fuel efficiency
  • Intends to explore lower-emission options as the business grows

6. Energy & Resource Use

Inside our facilities we aim to:

  • Use energy-efficient equipment where possible
  • Switch off or power-down equipment when not in use
  • Limit unnecessary printing and favour digital records
  • Encourage responsible water and resource use by staff

7. Staff Responsibilities

All RenovaTech staff and contractors are expected to:

  • Follow this Environmental Policy
  • Complete basic environmental and safe-handling training
  • Use correct procedures when managing hazardous materials
  • Support waste sorting, recycling and reuse practices in daily work

8. Working with Responsible Partners

We only work with recycling and disposal partners who:

  • Comply with Australian environmental laws and guidelines
  • Can provide transparency about their processes
  • Handle hazardous materials appropriately
  • Do not use unsafe or irresponsible e-waste export practices

9. Continuous Improvement & Review

RenovaTech sets internal environmental goals, such as:

  • Increasing refurbishment and reuse rates
  • Reducing landfill contributions
  • Improving recycling performance
  • Expanding donation and reuse programs

This policy and our environmental performance are reviewed regularly and updated as regulations, technologies and best practices evolve.

Data Destruction Policy

Last updated: November 2025 | Location: Victoria, Australia

At RenovaTech, we take data security seriously. Every device we handle is processed using strict security standards to ensure your data is permanently destroyed in a safe, compliant, and fully traceable manner.

1. Our Commitment to Data Security

RenovaTech follows recognised industry and legal standards, including:

  • Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)
  • Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)
  • NIST 800-88 Guidelines for Media Sanitisation
  • AS/NZS 5377:2013 E-Waste Management Standard

We never access, view, or attempt to recover your data. Our goal is simple: complete, irreversible destruction.

2. Approved Data Destruction Methods

2.1 Certified Software Wiping

We use professional wiping tools — such as Blancco, KillDisk, or similar — that meet the NIST 800-88 Clear/Purge standards.

This process includes:

  • Full overwrite wiping
  • Verification of successful erasure
  • Serial number logs
  • Issued Certificate of Data Destruction

2.2 Physical Destruction

If a device cannot be wiped or a client requests it, we use secure physical destruction methods:

  • Hard drive shredding
  • Crushing or disintegration
  • Degaussing (magnetic media)

This ensures data cannot be recovered under any circumstances.

3. Chain of Custody Protection

We maintain a secure, documented chain of custody for all devices:

  • Assets tagged and logged at collection
  • Locked, sealed containers used for transport
  • Devices stored in restricted-access, CCTV-monitored areas
  • Full pickup → processing → destruction tracking

You will always know where your devices are in the process.

4. Secure Handling & Staff Requirements

Only authorised RenovaTech staff can handle data-bearing devices.
All technicians must:

  • Hold a valid police check
  • Sign confidentiality agreements
  • Complete secure-handling training
  • Follow all security protocols

5. Certificates & Reporting

Upon completion, you will receive:

  • Certificate of Data Destruction
  • Asset serial numbers
  • Destruction method used (wipe/shred)
  • Technician name and timestamp

This documentation supports compliance, audit requirements, and internal records.

6. Secure Storage & Facility Protection

Before destruction, all devices are stored in:

  • Lockable cages or secure rooms
  • Restricted-access areas
  • CCTV-monitored environments
  • Alarm-protected facilities

High-security assets may be sealed in tamper-evident containers.

7. Incident Response

In the rare event of a suspected device or data issue:

  • The device is isolated immediately
  • Management is notified
  • A formal investigation is conducted
  • Clients are informed where legally required
  • Steps are taken to prevent future incidents

8. Continuous Improvement

We continuously review and improve our:

  • Wiping technologies
  • Destruction equipment
  • Staff training
  • Compliance standards
  • Reporting accuracy

This ensures RenovaTech remains a secure and reliable ITAD partner.
